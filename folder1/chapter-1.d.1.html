You hope it’s the latter; as sadistic as it sounds, it’s satisfying to watch the spirited lose their individuality. It’s like seeing someone else chained up in the same dungeon as you and getting the opportunity to say, ‘Now you understand.’
But that hope is something dull, like that of an adult flicking a penny into a wishing well. Whether the student is type one or two doesn’t ultimately affect you, and is therefore not interesting enough to be worth much thought. Nonetheless, you nods, putting on an interested facade.

To appear engaged, you asks, “Do you know her name?”
